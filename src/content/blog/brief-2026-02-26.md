## 🌐 Database Architectures & Vector Search

### 1. [PostgreSQL 19: The 'All-in-One' Database Era](https://www.postgresql.org/)
- **Signal Strength**: Very High | **Developer Engagement**: Massive
- **💡 Core Takeaway**: With native integration of advanced vector search capabilities, the need for specialized, standalone vector databases is plummeting. Foundational architectural shifts suggest Postgres is swallowing the database layer. Startups building on niche vector DBs should reconsider their technical debt.

### 2. [Surge in SQLite Adoption for Edge & Mobile AI](https://sqlite.org/)
- **Signal Strength**: High | **Discussion**: 1.2k+ comments
- **💡 Core Takeaway**: SQLite + Vector extensions are powering the new wave of "local-first" AI applications. As compute moves to the edge, data persistence must follow. The commercial viability of offline-first SaaS products is expanding rapidly.

### 3. [The Slow Death of ORMs? Raw SQL Makes a Comeback](https://news.ycombinator.com/)
- **Signal Strength**: Medium | **Market**: Backend Engineering
- **💡 Core Takeaway**: LLMs are remarkably good at generating raw, optimized SQL queries, drastically reducing the friction that ORMs historically solved. A new paradigm of "AI-assisted raw database interaction" is emerging, signaling a potential shift in full-stack curriculum and tooling.
