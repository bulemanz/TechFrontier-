## 🌐 Macro Trends in Edge Computing

### 1. [Apple's Open-Source MLX Framework Gains Enterprise Traction](https://github.com/ml-explore/mlx)
- **Signal Strength**: Extremely High | **Developer Engagement**: 45k+ Stars
- **💡 Core Takeaway**: We are observing a significant migration of inference workloads from cloud providers to local Apple Silicon hardware. MLX is lowering the barrier, threatening traditional cloud compute margins but unlocking privacy-first enterprise applications. Commercial viability for "local-first" AI startups is at an all-time high.

### 2. [WebGPU Now Default in Major Browsers: The End of Native Inference?](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/WebGPU_API)
- **Signal Strength**: High | **Discussion**: 850+ comments across dev hubs
- **💡 Core Takeaway**: WebGPU has crossed the chasm. Startups building in-browser AI agents no longer need complex backend GPU clusters. This shifts the bottleneck from server costs to frontend optimization. Expect a surge in lightweight, browser-based AI tooling in Q2 2026.

### 3. [The Rise of Small Language Models (SLMs) in IoT](https://huggingface.co/models)
- **Signal Strength**: Medium-High | **Market**: Industrial IoT
- **💡 Core Takeaway**: The obsession with AGI is masking a highly profitable niche: sub-2B parameter models running on constrained devices. Early commercial validation is strong in manufacturing and smart grid infrastructure.
